## 1. Context you should walk in knowing

### The center (not a typical daycare)
- **UW Children's Center at Portage Bay**, 3745 15th Ave NE, Seattle. Part of Haggard Childcare's UW-affiliated network.
- **138 children**, ages 3 months – 5 years. 2 infant rooms, 3 toddler, 4 preschool.
- **Ratios:** 3:1 infant, 5:1 toddler, 10:1 preschool → ~25–30 staff likely.
- **Pedagogy:** RIE (infants), Montessori (toddler/preschool). Both are documentation-heavy approaches — photos, observations, developmental notes matter operationally.
- **Tuition:** $2,220–$2,919/mo, 12 equal installments. Annual revenue ≈ **$4M+**. This is a mid-market center, not a mom-and-pop.
- **Population:** UW faculty/staff/students get priority via a waitpool. Likely complex billing: faculty payroll deduction? Student subsidies? GSA stipends? Sliding scale?

- **Hot spot:** 11 hr/day operating window with rolling check-in/out → sign-in/notification load is high.

### Why this matters for the conversation
She is **not** a single-location owner-operator. She likely sits inside a UW-adjacent or nonprofit umbrella with finance/HR systems (Workday?) above her, which means:
- Procare may be running *parallel* to a real ERP, not as the system of record for accounting.
- Budget approval, expense reimbursement, payroll likely live elsewhere → expense mgmt pain is real but Procare isn't the answer they tried.
- Family billing has university-specific edge cases (subsidies, payroll deduction).

## 2. Procare 30-second briefing (so you ask informed questions)

**What Procare is:** 30-yr-old market leader, ~40,000 centers. Two products that don't sync cleanly:
- **Procare Desktop** (legacy on-prem) — billing, accounting, reporting depth
- **Procare Online / Connect** — newer cloud product + parent app (check-in, photos, messaging)
- **Tuition Express** — payments rail (ACH + cards). Opaque, custom-quoted fees.

**Known pain points from public reviews (use as probes, don't lead with them):**
| Pain | What to listen for |
|---|---|
| Desktop ↔ Connect sync gaps | "We enter allergies/schedules twice" |
| Push notification latency | "Parents got the 11a diaper change ping at 6p" |
| Photo download/export limits | Yearbooks, graduation, parent off-boarding |
| Bank account changes need backend ticket (48hr+) | Anything requiring "I had to email support" |
| Renewal price hikes (one center: 323%) | "Our renewal jumped…" |
| CC fees opaque, vary card-to-card | Payments economics |
| Reporting/accounting lacks customization | "I export to Excel/QuickBooks every month" |
| Customer support wait times | "It took a week to…" |

### Competitive landscape (so you can place her stack)
- **Brightwheel** — parent-experience leader, ~$2–4/child/mo, 2.9% + $0.30 CC / 1% ACH (cap $5). Best billing UX in segment.
- **Lillio (HiMama)** — educator/curriculum-first, weaker billing.
- **Famly** — EU darling, collaborative feed, weak on US subsidies.
- **Kangarootime** — multi-site mid-market, competes head-on with Procare.
- **Adjacent gaps:** expense mgmt (receipts, classroom supply, petty cash, food vendors) almost always lives in QuickBooks/Xero/Ramp/Divvy — not in the childcare suite. **Suspected white space.**

## 3. Your 30-min agenda

| Min | Block | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 0–2 | Frame & thank | Re-anchor: "I want to learn your workflow, not pitch anything." |
| 2–7 | Her world | Role, day shape, team, what *she* personally owns vs. delegates |
| 7–15 | Tool inventory + switch story | Map the stack; uncover the last switch & why |
| 15–25 | Deep JTBD on top 2 pains | Pick the 2 she heat-flags; go narrative |
| 25–28 | Magic wand + scoring | Force prioritization |
| 28–30 | Ask + close | Permission to follow up; intro to peers |

**Rule of thumb:** When she says something interesting, **shut up and ask "tell me about the last time that happened."** Stories > opinions.

## 4. The interview script (questions in priority order)

### A. Opening frame (2 min)
> "Thanks again, Lauren. I'm doing this to *learn*, not sell. I'd love to understand how you actually run the back office at Portage Bay day-to-day. I'll ask a lot of 'walk me through the last time...' questions — that gets me much better data than hypotheticals. Mind if I take notes? Anything off-limits before we start?"

### B. Her world (5 min) — orient yourself

1. **"Walk me through your morning from arrival to 10am. What systems do you touch?"**
   *(Listen for: # of logins, where Procare appears, what's still on paper, the 7a check-in rush.)*
2. **"Who else on staff lives in the software? Teachers? An assistant director? A bookkeeper?"**
3. **"What's the split between the things only *you* can do vs. what you've delegated?"**
   *(Reveals where the bottleneck is — and who the real buyer is.)*

### C. Stack inventory (3 min) — get the map

4. **"If I made you list every piece of software the center pays for, what would be on the list?"**
   *(Probe specifically: Procare modules, payments, payroll, accounting (QB? UW Workday?), comms, food program, subsidy/state reporting, scheduling, HR/onboarding.)*
5. **"Which of those do you wish you could throw out tomorrow?"**

### D. Switch story (5 min) — the gold

JTBD interviews live or die on the **switch interview**. Pick whichever is true:

6a. **(If they recently adopted Procare):** "Take me back to the moment you decided to switch *to* Procare. What was happening that week? What was the last straw with the old system?"
6b. **(If they're long-time Procare):** "When was the last time you seriously considered switching *away* from Procare? What pulled you back?"

Follow-ups (Forces of Progress):
- **Push:** "What about the old way was painful enough to act?"
- **Pull:** "What made Procare feel like the answer?"
- **Anxiety:** "What worried you about switching?"
- **Habit:** "What did you almost stick with anyway?"

### E. Workflow deep-dives (10 min) — lead with scheduling, then pick 1–2 more

Heat-check each in 30 seconds, then go deep on the top 2. **Start with scheduling** — that's the live hypothesis from Lauren.

**(i) Staff scheduling, ratios, and call-outs** — 🔥 *PRIMARY HYPOTHESIS*
- "Walk me through how next week's staff schedule actually gets built. Who does it, in what tool, how long does it take?"
- "Tell me about the last time someone called out at 6:30am. What happens in the next 30 minutes?"
- "How do you keep ratio when a teacher goes home sick mid-day? Who's tracking that in real time?"
- "Does the scheduling tool know each teacher's certifications, hours-this-week, OT thresholds, and the rooms they're qualified for? Or is that in your head / a spreadsheet?"
- "If I told you an AI could draft next week's schedule for you to edit in 30 seconds — what would have to be true for you to trust it?"
- "What about subs — do you have a pool? How do you reach them at 6:30am?"
- 🎯 *Listen for: spreadsheets, group texts, sticky notes, mental load, the cost of being short-ratio (closed room? sent kids home? director-on-the-floor?). This is the wedge — get specific dollar/hour cost.*

**(ii) Monthly billing & payments**
- "Take me through the 1st of the month. Who clicks what? What breaks?"
- "How are faculty/staff/student rates and subsidies handled? Payroll deduction or direct pay?"
- "What's your collections process for late payment? Last awkward call?"
- "Card vs ACH mix? Who eats the processing fees?"
- 🎯 *Listen for: rate complexity, dunning, refund/credit handling, subsidy reconciliation, year-end tax statements.*

**(iii) Expense management** (hypothesized white-space)
- "Outside of tuition coming *in* — how do classroom supply purchases, food orders, petty cash, and teacher reimbursements get tracked?"
- "If a teacher buys $80 of art supplies at Target, what happens?"
- "How do you reconcile that at month-end? With whom? In what tool?"
- 🎯 *Procare doesn't do this. Likely QuickBooks + receipts + Excel + (Workday). The seam between ops and accounting.*

**(iv) Sign-in / check-in / pickup**
- "Walk me through a normal 5pm pickup. What goes wrong on a bad day?"
- "How do you handle an unauthorized pickup attempt? Show me the click-path."
- "Do parents check in on their own phone, a kiosk, or staff sign them in?"
- 🎯 *Listen for: latency, identity/verification, late pickup billing, sub teachers without logins.*

**(v) Parent notifications & photo sharing**
- "Last time a parent complained about the app, what was it about?"
- "How do teachers decide what to post? Is there a policy or norm?"
- "What happens to a child's photos when they leave the program?"
- 🎯 *Listen for: notification lag, content moderation burden, consent, archive/export pain.*

### F. Magic wand + forced prioritization (3 min)

7. **"If I gave you a magic wand that fixed *one* thing in your operations tomorrow, what would it be?"** *(First answer is real; don't accept "everything.")*
8. **"Of everything we discussed, rank your top 3 pains by how much time/money they cost you per month."**
9. **"If your software stayed exactly as buggy as today but cost half as much — would you switch? What if it were twice as good but cost 2x?"** *(Probes willingness-to-pay vs. switching cost.)*

### G. Close (2 min)

10. **"Who else at Portage Bay or in the Haggard network should I talk to? An assistant director, a teacher, your bookkeeper?"** *(Snowball — these are your next 5 interviews.)*
11. **"Can I come back to you in a few weeks once I've talked to 5–10 more directors? I'd love to show you what I'm seeing and get a sanity check."** *(Establishes ongoing relationship; she becomes design partner if this is real.)*
12. Offer to share back synthesis when you have it. Thank her.

## 5. What "success" looks like by the 30-min mark

You should leave with:
- [ ] Her **complete stack** named (every SaaS, every spreadsheet).
- [ ] At least **one switch story** with the trigger event, the alternatives considered, and what tipped the decision.
- [ ] **Top 3 ranked pains** with rough time/$ cost.
- [ ] Confirmation or rejection of the **AI scheduling hypothesis** (Lauren's live signal — is it universal across directors?).
- [ ] Confirmation or rejection of the **expense-mgmt seam hypothesis**.
- [ ] **2+ names** of people to interview next.
- [ ] Permission to **come back** in 2–4 weeks.

If you only leave with a list of Procare complaints and no story, you ran a feature interview, not a JTBD interview. Re-run with someone else.

## 6. Venture decision framework (post-interview, for you)

After this and ~5 more interviews, evaluate:

1. **Is the pain consolidated or fragmented?** A single dominant JTBD across centers (e.g., "make billing-day disappear") is venture-grade. A laundry list of small annoyances is not.
2. **Is there a real seam Procare *can't* close?** Procare has 30 yr of inertia. New entrant wins on (a) a workflow they architecturally can't reach (mobile-first parent UX → Brightwheel did this) or (b) a category they ignore (expense + accounting integration is my prior).
3. **Buyer = User?** Director buys, but if teachers/parents are the users, the product motion is bottoms-up (Brightwheel) vs. top-down (Procare). Pick one.
4. **Unit economics:** At $3/child/mo SaaS + 1% ACH on $4M/yr tuition = ~$5K/yr SaaS + $40K/yr payments per center like Portage Bay. Payments is the business. SaaS is the wedge.
5. **Distribution moat:** Childcare directors trust other directors. State licensing rep networks, NAEYC, regional associations are the real channel. Without one, you'll burn out doing 1:1 sales.

**Kill criteria** (be honest): If Lauren says "Procare is annoying but I'd never switch — too much risk," and 4 of 5 other directors say the same, the *switching cost* is your real enemy, not Procare's product. That's a much harder venture than it looks.

## 7. Things to NOT do

- Don't pitch your idea. You don't have one yet, and pitching contaminates the data.
- Don't lead with "do you have problems with X?" — she'll be polite. Ask for stories.
- Don't say "Procare sucks, right?" Even if she agrees, it's worthless data.
- Don't ask hypotheticals ("would you pay for X?"). Ask history ("when did you last pay for something to fix this?").
- Don't fill silence. Count to 5 after she stops talking. The best stuff comes after the pause.
