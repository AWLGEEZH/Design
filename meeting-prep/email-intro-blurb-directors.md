# Cold-intro blurb for incoming Haggard directors
**Context:** Lauren Wenham emailed her director network introducing me as "a parent who works at Microsoft on UX." This is the template reply when one of them reaches out.

**Framing notes:**
- Match Lauren's framing: curious parent + UX background, not founder pitch.
- Lead with listening, not building. "If a clear pattern shows up" is the conditional that earns trust.
- Ask for the meeting at the end with a soft calendar hook, not a Calendly link (too transactional for a warm intro).

---

**Subject:** Intro from Lauren — would love to learn how you run things

Hi [Name],

Thanks so much for reaching out — Lauren mentioned she'd put my name in front of you.

Quick intro: I'm a parent at Portage Bay (my kid has been there since [age/year]). My day job is at Microsoft, working on making products easier for people to use. After a recent conversation with Lauren about the patchwork of tools she uses to run the center, I got curious whether anyone has really sat down and asked directors what would actually make your day better — not pitched you something, just listened. So that's what I'm doing.

If you're up for it, I'd love 30 minutes of your time to hear how you run things day-to-day, what drives you crazy, and what you wish existed. No agenda beyond learning. If a clear pattern shows up across the directors I talk to, I'll come back and share what I'm seeing — and from there we can figure out if it's worth building anything.

What does your calendar look like over the next couple of weeks?

Thanks,
Doyle
